How to Use This.
No Overwhelm.

You don't need to change everything at once. Start with one thing. Build from there. This is the exact progression that makes it stick.

01

Figure out where you are right now

Day 1 is always the first day of full bleeding. Count forward. Not sure? Estimate based on your last period start date — even a rough guess gets you started. Your phases are approximate to begin with. The tracking refines over time.

🩸 Days 1–5 · Menstrual · Winter
🌱 Days 6–13 · Follicular · Spring
☀️ Days 14–17 · Ovulatory · Summer
🍂 Days 18–28 · Luteal · Autumn
02

Pick one small thing for your phase — just one

Not the whole meal plan. Not a new workout program. The smallest possible thing that puts you in relationship with your body today. Here's where to start based on where you are:

🩸 Menstrual — Rest without apologizing. Wear socks today. Drink warm water or tea instead of cold. Let yourself be slow. This is not laziness — this is your body doing significant work. Rest is the practice.
🌱 Follicular — Start something. Go outside for 20 minutes. Reach out to someone you've been meaning to connect with. Try the thing you've been putting off. Your energy is building — use the beginning of it on something that lights you up.
☀️ Ovulatory — Show up. Make the call. Have the conversation. Schedule what matters this week for today. Your communication and confidence are at their natural peak right now — use that window intentionally.
🍂 Luteal — Nourish and protect your energy. Eat a raw carrot today (it helps process excess estrogen). Take magnesium glycinate before bed. Say no to one optional obligation. Your threshold is lower — honor that instead of pushing through.
03

Build from there — one layer per cycle

Once the small things feel natural, layer in the next thing. Here's the order that works — each one builds on the last:

📅
Tracking first. Notice what shifts and when. You can't optimize what you haven't observed yet. Even one note per day tells you everything after two or three cycles.
🥗
Then eating. Start with just the phase you're entering next. One grocery swap. One phase-aligned meal. Expand from there — no need to overhaul everything at once.
💪
Then movement. Match your workout intensity to your actual energy — not what you planned when you felt different. Rest in menstrual, build in follicular, peak in ovulatory, slow in luteal.
💼
Then your schedule and work. Protect your low-energy phases — say no, simplify, create space. Schedule hard decisions, big meetings, and creative pushes in your follicular and ovulatory phases. This one changes everything.

✦ The Four Phases

Your cycle mirrors the seasons.
Each phase has its own intelligence.

Understanding which phase you're in is the foundation of everything. Once you know, the body's language becomes clear.

✦ Winter · Days 1–5 approx
Menstrual Phase
Rest + Reflection + Renewal
RestGentle movementWarm foodsMineralsJournaling

The body is shedding and renewing. Energy naturally turns inward. This is not a time to push — it's a time to receive. Rest here is productive. Warmth, nourishment, and quiet reflection support what the body is already doing. What needs to be released? What clarity arises in the stillness?

✦ Spring · Days 6–13 approx
Follicular Phase
Growth + Creation + New Beginnings
CreativityStrength trainingNew projectsFresh foodsLearning

Estrogen rises and energy returns. This is the beginning of a new cycle — full of creative potential and clarity. The mind feels sharper, initiative comes more naturally, and new beginnings feel genuinely exciting. An excellent time to start things, set intentions, and build physical strength.

✦ Summer · Days 14–17 approx
Ovulatory Phase
Connection + Expression + Peak Energy
CommunicationSocial energyPeak movementCollaborationConfidence

The shortest phase — and often the most electric. Natural charisma and communication are at their peak. Energy is highest. This is the time for visibility: important meetings, hard conversations, collaborations, and putting yourself out there. Your magnetism is genuinely at its most powerful.

✦ Autumn · Days 18–28 approx
Luteal Phase
Grounding + Completion + Inner Work
BoundariesPMS supportOrganizationNervous system careSlowing down

Progesterone rises, and energy begins to draw inward again. Attention to detail is heightened — excellent for editing, organizing, and completing projects. As the phase progresses, the body needs more support: blood sugar balance, nervous system regulation, and honoring the need to slow down. PMS often lives here — and almost always has a nutritional root.

✦ The Hormone Education Hub

Before adding more.
Support the foundations.

Most hormone imbalance isn't a mystery — it has roots. Cycle Harmony teaches you where to look first, starting with what you're already surrounded by.

🌿

Environment + Endocrine Disruptors

Endocrine disruptors interfere with your hormones at a cellular level — and most people don't know what they're actually exposed to daily. Small swaps, not fear.

  • Plastics & BPA in food storage
  • Synthetic fragrances + aerosols
  • Conventional cleaning products
  • Non-stick cookware (PFAS)
  • Pesticide residue on produce
  • Scented candles + air fresheners
☀️

Circadian Rhythm Support

Light is information. Your hormones are deeply tied to your body clock — and most people are disrupting it daily without realizing it. Morning sunlight is one of the most powerful free tools you have.

  • Morning sunlight within 30 min of waking
  • Consistent sleep and wake times
  • Evening screen use (blue light)
  • Rest as hormonal support, not laziness
🫁

Stress + Nervous System

Cortisol and progesterone compete for the same precursor. Chronic stress is a direct hormonal disruptor — and it's the most overlooked one. Safety is a hormonal signal.

  • Breathwork practices daily
  • Meditation & nervous system rest
  • Journaling for emotional regulation
  • Slowing down as a health practice
🩸

Blood Sugar Balance

Blood sugar dysregulation is one of the most common and least-discussed drivers of hormone imbalance. It shows up as PMS, cravings, mid-cycle energy crashes, and irregular cycles.

  • Protein + healthy fats at every meal
  • Never eat carbs alone (pair everything)
  • Walk after meals — even 10 minutes
  • Cravings are information, not failure

✦ Traditional Wisdom

What Ancient Practitioners
Knew First

Before labs and supplements, Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and ancestral herbalists had a deep understanding of the female body. Much of what they practiced, modern research is only beginning to confirm.

🔥

Warmth as Medicine

TCM teaches that cold contracts and warmth opens. The uterus thrives with warmth — cold environments, cold foods, and cold extremities during menstruation can increase cramping and slow blood flow.

Keep your feet warm during your period. Wear socks. This is not old-fashioned — it's circulatory support.
🌸

The Womb as Center

In many traditional systems, the womb is considered the energetic and physical center of the female body — the seat of creativity, intuition, and life-force. Protecting the torso and abdomen, especially during menstruation, is considered basic self-care.

Cover your torso in cold weather. Avoid iced drinks during your period. Warmth supports your cycle.
🌊

Blood Nourishment

TCM views menstruation as a monthly loss that must be replenished. Nourishing blood through food (dark leafy greens, beets, bone broth, red meat) is foundational — not optional. This maps directly onto modern understanding of iron and mineral replenishment.

After your period, prioritize iron-rich and blood-building foods for the first week of follicular phase.

✦ Womb Warmth Practices

Wear socks — especially during menstruation. Cold feet = cold pelvis in TCM.
Cover your lower abdomen in cold weather. Keep a wrap or sweater tied at your waist.
Drink warm or room-temperature water and teas throughout your period — not iced.
Warm baths with Epsom salt during the luteal phase to ease cramps and relax the nervous system.
Eat cooked, warm foods during menstruation rather than raw salads or cold smoothies.
Use a heating pad or hot water bottle on your lower abdomen during your period.
Morning warm lemon water instead of cold — especially during menstrual and luteal phases.

✦ Supplement Support

What most women are
actually missing.

Food first — always. But these are the supplements with the most consistent support for hormonal health, cycle regulation, and overall wellbeing. Quality matters more than quantity.

☀️

Vitamin D3 + K2

D3 supports immune function, mood stability, hormone health, and insulin sensitivity. K2 ensures calcium goes to bones, not arteries. These two always go together.

Daily · with a fatty meal
🧲

Magnesium Glycinate

One of the most common deficiencies. Reduces PMS, eases cramps and breast tenderness, supports sleep, blood sugar balance, and nervous system regulation. Highly bioavailable form.

Evening · especially luteal phase
🦠

Probiotic

Your gut microbiome directly influences estrogen metabolism through the estrobolome. A disrupted gut (from antibiotics, diet, or stress) can cause estrogen recirculation and hormonal imbalance.

Daily · Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium
⚡

B12 + B Complex

B vitamins support energy production, mood regulation, nervous system health, and estrogen metabolism. B6 specifically reduces PMS symptoms, bloating, and mood swings. B12 supports energy.

Morning · with food
🐟

Omega-3 (Fish Oil)

Anti-inflammatory fat that directly supports hormone production. Reduces period pain, improves mood, and supports the liver's ability to process and eliminate excess estrogen.

Daily · with the largest meal
🌿

Adaptogens

Holy basil, reishi, and cordyceps — not ashwagandha if you have autoimmune concerns. Support cortisol, energy, immune modulation, and gut health. Gentler and safer for sensitive systems.

Reishi evening · Cordyceps morning

🦠 Rebuilding After Antibiotics

Antibiotics don't just kill harmful bacteria — they wipe out beneficial strains that are essential for hormone health, mood, immunity, and digestion. Rebuilding takes intention and time. Start with a diverse, fiber-rich diet and a high-quality probiotic. Then layer in fermented foods as your gut stabilizes.

Sauerkraut Kimchi Greek yogurt Kefir Bone broth Diverse vegetables Garlic + onion Flaxseed Chia seeds Quality probiotic

Give yourself 3–6 months of consistent support. Your gut and your hormones will rebuild together — they're deeply interconnected.
