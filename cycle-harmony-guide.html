✦ Chapter 1

Understanding Your
Hormone Cycle

Your menstrual cycle is a 21–35 day process driven by four shifting hormones. It's not just about your period — it's a monthly rhythm that influences your energy, mood, focus, metabolism, creativity, and relational needs every single day.

The four key hormones

Estrogen: Rises through follicular and peaks at ovulation. Boosts mood, energy, and libido. Supports brain function and bone density.
Progesterone: Rises after ovulation through luteal. Calming, grounding, sleep-supportive. Drops sharply before your period — this drop drives PMS.
LH (luteinizing hormone): Surges right before ovulation, triggering egg release.
FSH (follicle-stimulating hormone): Rises during follicular phase, stimulating egg development.

How to identify which phase you're in

✦ Winter · Days 1–5 approx
Menstrual
Rest + Reflection + Renewal

Your period has started. Estrogen and progesterone are both at their lowest. Energy turns inward. You may feel tired, introspective, and craving warmth and rest. This is biologically appropriate — your body is doing significant work.

Signs
  • Bleeding (Day 1)
  • Low energy
  • Wanting quiet
  • Craving warmth
Support
  • Rest without guilt
  • Warm foods + tea
  • Gentle movement
  • Reflection + journal
✦ Spring · Days 6–13 approx
Follicular
Growth + Creation + Energy Rising

Bleeding has stopped. Estrogen begins rising. Energy returns, mood lifts, and motivation builds. This is the best phase for starting new things, building strength, and creative work. Your body is building toward ovulation.

Signs
  • Increasing energy
  • Clearer skin
  • Optimism
  • Mucus increasing
Support
  • Strength training
  • New projects
  • Fresh foods
  • Learn something
✦ Summer · Days 14–17 approx
Ovulatory
Connection + Peak Energy + Expression

Estrogen peaks. LH surges and the egg is released. Natural charisma, social confidence, and communication are at their highest. The shortest phase — and often the most magnetic. Schedule big moments here.

Signs
  • Peak energy
  • Egg-white mucus
  • Libido increase
  • Feeling magnetic
Support
  • Peak workouts
  • Big meetings
  • Social plans
  • Collaboration
✦ Autumn · Days 18–28 approx
Luteal
Grounding + Completion + Inner Work

Progesterone rises, energy draws inward. Excellent for finishing projects, organizing, and detailed work. As progesterone drops toward your period, PMS can arise — almost always with a nutritional root. This phase needs the most nourishment.

Signs
  • Lower energy
  • More sensitive
  • Cravings increase
  • Bloating possible
Support
  • Pilates + walking
  • Finish + organize
  • Magnesium foods
  • Protect your no

How to start tracking your cycle

Day 1 is always the first day of full menstrual bleeding. Mark it on your calendar or in an app. From there, count forward. Most cycles are 25–35 days. Track daily: energy (1–10), mood, sleep quality, cravings, symptoms, and any notable physical observations. After 2–3 cycles, patterns emerge that are uniquely yours.

✦ The Foundation Daily Tea Blend

Nettle Leaf · Raspberry Leaf · Holy Basil · Lemon Balm · Spearmint
Steep 1–2 tsp per herb in hot (not boiling) water for 5–10 minutes. Drink 1–2 cups daily. This covers minerals, stress support, uterine nourishment, and nervous system calming all in one cup.

✦ Chapter 4

Traditional Wisdom &
Womb Warmth

Before labs and supplements, Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and ancestral herbalists understood something modern medicine is only beginning to confirm: the female body runs on warmth, rhythm, and nourishment.

🔥 Warmth as Medicine

TCM teaches that cold contracts and warmth opens. The uterus thrives with warmth — cold environments, cold beverages, and cold extremities during menstruation are associated with increased cramping and disrupted blood flow. This isn't just tradition — poor peripheral circulation genuinely affects pelvic blood flow.

🌸 Blood Nourishment

TCM views menstruation as a monthly loss that must be actively replenished. Nourishing blood through food — dark leafy greens, beets, bone broth, red meat — is foundational. This maps directly to modern understanding of iron, B12, and mineral replenishment after blood loss.

🌊 Qi Flow + Rest

In TCM, the menstrual phase is a time of significant Qi (life force) movement. Overexertion during this time is said to deplete Qi and create stagnation — which presents as worse cramping, clots, and fatigue in subsequent cycles. Rest is not laziness here: it's energetic stewardship.

🍂 Seasonal Living

Your cycle mirrors the seasons of nature: Menstrual = Winter (rest, inward, release), Follicular = Spring (growth, new starts), Ovulatory = Summer (peak, expression), Luteal = Autumn (harvest, completion, letting go). When you live seasonally within your cycle, effort and rest are always in the right places.

Womb Warmth Practices

Wear socks during your period — especially in cold weather. Cold feet = cold pelvis in TCM.
Keep your lower abdomen covered. A scarf tied at your waist is the simplest protection.
Drink warm or room-temp water and tea during menstruation — avoid iced drinks.
Warm baths with Epsom salt in the luteal phase ease cramps and calm the nervous system.
Eat cooked, warm foods during your period — not raw salads or cold smoothies.
Use a heating pad or hot water bottle on your lower abdomen when cramping.
Morning warm lemon water — especially during menstrual and luteal phases.
Cover your torso in cold weather. Your core warmth affects your reproductive warmth.

✦ Chapter 5

Supplement &
Gut Health Support

Food first — always. These are the supplements with the most consistent support for hormonal health and cycle regulation. Quality over quantity. You don't need everything at once.

SupplementWhy It Matters for HormonesWhen to Take
☀️ Vitamin D3 + K2 D3 supports immune function, mood, insulin sensitivity, and hormone production. K2 ensures calcium goes to bones — always take these together. Most women are deficient. Daily · fatty meal
🧲 Magnesium Glycinate Reduces PMS, breast tenderness, cramping, and anxiety. Supports sleep quality and blood sugar regulation. One of the most impactful supplements for hormone balance — and one of the most depleted. Evening · luteal especially
🦠 Probiotic The estrobolome (gut bacteria) directly processes estrogen. A disrupted microbiome — from antibiotics, diet, or stress — causes estrogen recirculation and imbalance. Look for Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium strains. Daily · consistent timing
⚡ B12 + B Complex B6 reduces PMS, bloating, and mood swings. B12 supports energy and nervous system health. B2 supports mitochondrial energy production. Essential for women who don't eat much red meat or are gluten-free. Morning · with food
🐟 Omega-3 (Fish Oil) Anti-inflammatory fat that directly supports hormone production. Reduces period pain, improves mood stability, and supports the liver's ability to process and eliminate excess estrogen efficiently. Daily · largest meal

🦠 Rebuilding After Antibiotics

Antibiotics wipe out both harmful and beneficial bacteria — including strains essential for hormone metabolism, mood, immunity, and digestion. Rebuilding takes 3–6 months of consistent support. Start with diverse fiber, a quality probiotic, and bone broth. Layer in fermented foods (sauerkraut, kimchi, kefir) as your gut stabilizes. Avoid sugar and processed foods while rebuilding — they feed the bacteria you don't want back first.
